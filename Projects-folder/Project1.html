Web Site Critiques

The Questions

  1. What was your first impression of this site?

    Answer: I liked it lots of colors, different fonts very eye appealing

  2. How does this site organize the flow of information?(Do they have links on the left and text on the right> SOme other scheme? No scheme?)

    Answer: Its pretty organized there are lots of links to easily navigate where to go.

  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?

    Answer: It makes sense like I mentioned they provide the main things you would need right on the front page

  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?

    Answer: I think it enhances it. Very much a Trader Joes vibe. I think that if it was a smiths website it would be a little overwhelming.

  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?

    Answer: It took little to no time to load after clicking the link.

  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a backround color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?

    Answer: The font size and color is good. All very easy to read I think that the font enhances the website very eye catching. The graphics and images are also very eye catching very Trader Joes vibes.

  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?

    Answer: Yes they have a navigation bar to the side that is very helpful. But also when you click it it brings you to more things.

  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?

    Answer: Yes mostly browns, reds, and blues. It was repeated on all pages.

  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive desgin)?

    Answer: It responded well minimized the information to what you would need in that small of a window.

  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

    Answer: They offer podcasts and recipes which I feel like is unique. Ecspecially the podcasts.
